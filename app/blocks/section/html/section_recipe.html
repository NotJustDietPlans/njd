Kalamata Chicken with Summer Fruit Salad

Mixing mango with shrimp may not seem a good idea, unless you’re aware of how tasty and mouthwatering this dish is. The delicious mango complements the seafood perfectly, and the lime adds just a little bitterness to an already heavenly delicious recipe

Best For: Carbohydrate Metabolism Type

Ingredients

  • 4 skinless, split, bone-in (trimmed, about about 1/2 pounds) Chicken Breasts
  • 1 medium (seeded and cut into 8 wedges) Red Bell Pepper
  • 1 medium (seeded and cut into 8 wedges) Orange Bell Pepper
  • 1 medium (cut into 8 wedges) Gold Potatoes
  • 1 medium (peeled and cut into 8 wedges) Red Onion
  • 40 pitted (smashed, about about 1/3 cups) Kalamata Olives
  • 1 tbsp Olive Oil
  • Grated zest and juice of of Lemon
  • 1 tbsp minced Garlic
  • 1 tbsp chopped fresh Oregano
  • 3/4 tsp freshly ground Black Pepper
  • 3/4 tsp Paprika
  • 4 (1 banana ~= 4.5 oz) Bananas
  • 20 Strawberry

Directions to prepare:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F.
  2. Tear off 2 sheets of nonstick aluminum foil, each 24" long. Put the dull sides together, and fold over the edge on one side twice, to make a seam. Open up, and line and cover the edges of a 17" by 12" rimmed baking pan.
  3. Place the chicken on one side of the pan and the bell peppers, potatoes, onion, and olives on the other side. In a medium bowl, whisk together the oil, lemon zest and juice, garlic, oregano, salt, black pepper, and paprika. Drizzle over the chicken and the vegetables, and toss to coat.
  4. Roast for 40 to 45 minutes, turning the chicken and stirring the vegetables halfway through cooking, or until a meat thermometer registers 165°F when inserted into the thickest part of the chicken. Arrange 1 chicken breast and a quarter of the vegetables on 4 plates.
